Joseph Dacre Carlyle (Carlisle, June 4, 1758 - Newcastle upon Tyne, April 12, 1804) is an English orientalist who, after studying at Cambridge University, became a professor of Arabic. In 1799, he was attached by the British government to Lord Elgin's embassy in the Ottoman Empire.
